# PROBABILITY MODEL

## Purpose

This document defines how Project Hunter estimates probability.

The objective is not to predict exact prices.

The objective is to estimate the probability distribution of future outcomes.

Project Hunter must always think in probabilities, not certainties.

---

# First Principle

A project is not evaluated by one expected price.

A project is evaluated by its full outcome distribution.

The system must estimate both upside and failure risk.

---

# Required Probability Outputs

Every project analysis must estimate:

- Probability of 2x
- Probability of 5x
- Probability of 10x
- Probability of 20x
- Probability of 50x
- Probability of 100x
- Probability of permanent capital impairment
- Probability of thesis failure
- Probability of market repricing

---

# Probability Sources

Probabilities must be derived from evidence, including:

- Historical analogs
- Market cap expansion potential
- Tokenomics
- Smart money accumulation
- Narrative timing
- Catalyst strength
- Liquidity conditions
- Market cycle position
- Developer strength
- Adoption growth
- Revenue growth
- Competitive landscape
- Supply pressure
- Whale behavior
- Exchange access
- Social attention
- Search trend acceleration

---

# No False Precision

Never pretend probabilities are exact.

Every probability must include a confidence level.

Example:

10x probability: 12–18%

Confidence: Medium

Reason:

The project has strong narrative alignment and low market cap, but tokenomics and liquidity remain uncertain.

---

# Expected Asymmetric Return

Every project must calculate Expected Asymmetric Return (EAR).

EAR is not the same as expected price.

EAR measures whether the upside tail is large enough to justify the downside risk.

---

# Probability Buckets

Use probability buckets instead of false certainty.

Suggested buckets:

- Very Low
- Low
- Moderate
- High
- Very High

When numerical estimates are used, they must be shown as ranges.

---

# Scenario Model

Every project must include at least four scenarios:

## Failure Case

The thesis breaks.

Capital loss is severe.

## Bear Case

The project survives but underperforms.

## Base Case

The project performs in line with realistic evidence.

## Asymmetric Bull Case

The market massively reprices the project.

---

# Time Horizons

Probabilities must be estimated across multiple time horizons:

- 6 months
- 12 months
- 24 months
- Full cycle

Short-term probabilities must be separated from cycle-level probabilities.

---

# Repricing Logic

The system must explain what event could cause repricing.

Examples:

- Major exchange listing
- Revenue inflection
- Whale accumulation
- Supply shock
- Narrative shift
- Institutional adoption
- Protocol upgrade
- Market cycle rotation
- Competitor failure

No repricing catalyst means lower probability.

---

# Failure Probability

Every project must estimate failure probability.

Failure may include:

- Permanent capital loss
- Token becoming irrelevant
- Product failure
- Team failure
- Tokenomics failure
- Regulatory failure
- Narrative failure
- Liquidity collapse

Failure probability must never be ignored.

---

# Probability Updating

Probabilities must update whenever new evidence appears.

Examples:

- New whale accumulation
- Revenue growth
- Unlock pressure
- Developer decline
- Major listing
- Major partnership
- Broken roadmap
- Regulatory action

Probabilities are dynamic.

Never fixed.

---

# Historical Calibration

Probability estimates must be calibrated against historical winners and failures.

The system must ask:

How often did similar projects produce 10x?

How often did similar projects fail?

How often did similar narratives become dominant?

How often did similar tokenomics destroy upside?

---

# Final Output

Every probability report must include:

- Upside probability table
- Failure probability
- Key assumptions
- Evidence supporting upside
- Evidence supporting failure
- Confidence level
- What would increase probability
- What would decrease probability

---

# Final Principle

Project Hunter does not need certainty.

It needs superior probability judgment.

The goal is not to always be right.

The goal is to find opportunities where the upside tail is mispriced by the market.
