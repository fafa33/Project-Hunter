# BACKTEST PROTOCOL

## Purpose

This document defines how Project Hunter validates itself.

A prediction engine is useless if it cannot correctly identify historical winners before they became obvious.

Project Hunter must continuously prove that its methodology works.

Backtesting is mandatory.

No model is considered valid without passing historical validation.

---

# First Principle

The system must never evaluate itself using today's knowledge.

Every historical project must be reconstructed exactly as it existed BEFORE its major appreciation.

Future information is forbidden.

No hindsight bias.

---

# Historical Test Set

The system must backtest against multiple market cycles.

Examples include:

- Bitcoin

- BNB
- Chainlink
- Solana
- Maker

- Injective
- Kaspa

Additional historical winners should continuously be added.

---

# Historical Reconstruction

For every project reconstruct:

Technology maturity

Developer activity

GitHub status

Revenue

Users

Exchange listings

Liquidity

Tokenomics

Inflation

FDV

Unlock schedule

Community size

Search trends

Narrative

VC ownership

Whale activity

Regulatory environment

Macro conditions

Everything must reflect ONLY the information available at that historical date.

---

# Blind Evaluation

The scoring engine must evaluate projects without knowing future prices.

The objective is to simulate real investment decisions.

Future appreciation must never influence the score.

---

# Ranking Test

For every historical date:

Generate a ranked list.

Determine whether the future winner appears near the top.

The objective is not perfect ranking.

The objective is consistently identifying exceptional opportunities.

---

# Failure Analysis

Whenever the model fails, identify:

Which signals were missing?

Which signals were misleading?

Which weights were incorrect?

Which assumptions failed?

Every failure becomes training data.

---

# False Positives

Identify projects that looked promising but failed.

Examples:

EOS

ICP

LUNA

Terra ecosystem

Helium

SafeMoon

Bitconnect

Other failed narratives

Determine why the model should reject them.

---

# False Negatives

Identify historical winners the model failed to detect.

Examples:

Bitcoin

Ethereum

Chainlink

Solana

Render

Kaspa

Hyperliquid

Determine why they were missed.

Improve the model accordingly.

---

# Cross-Cycle Validation

The model must perform consistently across multiple market cycles.

Bull markets

Bear markets

Sideways markets

High liquidity environments

Low liquidity environments

Changing macroeconomic conditions

---

# Continuous Learning

Every completed market cycle becomes new training data.

Historical databases must continuously expand.

The model must evolve after every cycle.

---

# Acceptance Criteria

Project Hunter should consistently rank future major winners near the top before their explosive appreciation.

If this objective is not achieved,

the model must be redesigned.

No exceptions.

---

# Final Principle

Backtesting is more important than prediction.

A model that cannot explain the past should never be trusted to predict the future.

Evidence always overrides intuition.

Historical validation always overrides confidence.
