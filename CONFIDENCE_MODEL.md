# CONFIDENCE MODEL

## Purpose

The Confidence Model measures the reliability of every conclusion produced by Project Hunter.

The objective is not increasing confidence.

The objective is accurately measuring uncertainty.

Confidence is earned through evidence.

Never through conviction.

---

# First Principle

A correct conclusion with low confidence is acceptable.

A confident conclusion without evidence is unacceptable.

Hunter must always distinguish between facts, evidence, assumptions and speculation.

---

# Confidence Sources

Confidence is determined by:

Data Quality

Evidence Quantity

Evidence Consistency

Historical Validation

Data Freshness

Independent Verification

On-chain Confirmation

Financial Transparency

Market Confirmation

Unknown Variables

---

# Evidence Classification

Every statement must be classified as one of:

Verified Fact

Strong Evidence

Moderate Evidence

Weak Evidence

Assumption

Speculation

Unknown

No conclusion may rely primarily on assumptions.

---

# Confidence Levels

Hunter uses five confidence levels.

Very High

High

Medium

Low

Very Low

Every report must assign one.

---

# Confidence Factors

Evaluate:

Research Completeness

Financial Transparency

Revenue Visibility

Developer Transparency

Tokenomics Clarity

Whale Visibility

Historical Comparisons

Macro Stability

Narrative Stability

Risk Visibility

---

# Confidence Penalties

Reduce confidence when:

Important data is missing.

Revenue cannot be verified.

Whale behavior is unclear.

Tokenomics are uncertain.

Roadmap is vague.

Competition is poorly understood.

Too many assumptions exist.

---

# Confidence Boosters

Increase confidence when:

Revenue is independently verified.

On-chain data confirms adoption.

Multiple data sources agree.

Historical analogs are strong.

Smart money confirms the thesis.

Developer execution is consistent.

The business model is simple and measurable.

---

# Unknown Variables

Every report must explicitly list:

Unknown facts

Missing data

Unverifiable assumptions

Open questions

Potential blind spots

Unknowns reduce confidence.

---

# Confidence vs Conviction

Conviction is personal.

Confidence is measurable.

Hunter reports confidence.

Users decide conviction.

---

# Dynamic Confidence

Confidence changes as new evidence appears.

Increase confidence when evidence improves.

Reduce confidence when uncertainty grows.

Never anchor to previous conclusions.

---

# Final Output

Every report must include:

Overall Confidence

Reasons for Confidence

Reasons for Uncertainty

Missing Information

Evidence Strength

Unknown Variables

Recommended Next Research Steps

---

# Final Principle

Hunter does not claim certainty.

Hunter measures confidence.

The objective is not sounding confident.

The objective is being accurate about uncertainty.
