# Project Hunter

> Building an AI-powered research system to discover asymmetric crypto opportunities before the market recognizes them.

---

# Mission

Project Hunter is not a crypto screener.

It is not a portfolio manager.

It is not a coin ranking website.

Its only mission is to discover crypto projects that have a statistically significant probability of becoming future asymmetric winners before they become obvious to the market.

The objective is to identify projects capable of producing extraordinary returns (10x–100x) while they are still largely ignored.

---

# Philosophy

History shows that the biggest crypto winners were rarely obvious.

Bitcoin

Ethereum

Chainlink

Solana

Render

Bittensor

Hyperliquid

...all looked uncertain before becoming market leaders.

Project Hunter exists to reconstruct the conditions that existed before those explosions.

Instead of asking:

"Which projects are good?"

Project Hunter asks:

"What characteristics existed before previous 10x–100x winners, and which current projects most closely match those conditions?"

---

# Core Principles

Project Hunter never ranks projects by popularity.

Project Hunter never follows influencers.

Project Hunter never assumes the market is efficient.

Every project must earn its score through measurable evidence.

No emotional bias.

No confirmation bias.

No tribalism.

Only evidence.

---

# Primary Objective

Estimate whether the market is significantly underpricing a project's future probability distribution.

The goal is NOT to maximize prediction accuracy.

The goal is to maximize asymmetric opportunity.

# Guiding Question

Every analysis must answer one question:

"Is the market materially underestimating this project's probability of becoming a future market leader?"

If the answer is no,

the project is rejected.

---

# Design Philosophy

This repository is designed to evolve into an autonomous research engine.

The final system should continuously:

- scan the crypto market
- collect new data
- update historical models
- detect pricing errors
- identify emerging narratives
- monitor smart money
- score opportunities
- explain every conclusion with evidence

---

# Rules

Evidence always wins.

Historical patterns matter.

Predictions without evidence are rejected.

Extraordinary claims require extraordinary evidence.

Unknown is better than false certainty.

The system must always challenge its own conclusions before accepting them.
