# PORTFOLIO ENGINE

## Purpose

The Portfolio Engine transforms research into portfolio decisions.

Finding exceptional opportunities is only half the process.

The other half is capital allocation.

The objective is maximizing long-term Expected Asymmetric Return while controlling permanent capital loss.

---

# First Principle

Not every good project deserves the same allocation.

Portfolio weight must reflect:

Expected return

Probability of success

Confidence level

Risk

Liquidity

Market conditions

---

# Portfolio Categories

Every project belongs to one category.

Exceptional Conviction

High Conviction

Core Position

Satellite Position

Watchlist

Research Only

Reject

---

# Position Size

Position size depends on:

Hunter Score

Confidence Score

Probability Model

Risk Score

Liquidity

Macro Environment

Market Cycle

Never allocate based only on excitement.

---

# Diversification

Diversification should reduce risk.

It should not reduce opportunity.

Avoid unnecessary duplication.

Examples:

Five Layer 1 projects are not diversification.

Three Oracle projects are not diversification.

Five AI projects are not diversification.

Diversify by investment thesis.

---

# Correlation

Estimate correlation between holdings.

Examples:

Layer 1

AI

DePIN

Infrastructure

Payments

Stablecoins

Gaming

Privacy

Oracles

High correlation reduces portfolio quality.

---

# Capital Allocation

Capital should gradually move toward:

Higher conviction

Higher evidence

Higher asymmetry

Lower permanent downside

---

# Dynamic Allocation

Portfolio weights are dynamic.

Increase allocation when:

Evidence improves

Revenue accelerates

Smart money accumulates

Risk decreases

Probability increases

Reduce allocation when:

Risk increases

Whales distribute

Revenue weakens

Narrative collapses

Investment thesis weakens

---

# Cash Management

Holding cash is a valid decision.

Hunter is never forced to remain fully invested.

Cash becomes an asset when opportunities are scarce.

---

# Portfolio Review

Review portfolio regularly.

Questions include:

Does every position still deserve capital?

Has a better opportunity appeared?

Has risk increased?

Has expected return declined?

---

# Portfolio Metrics

Track:

Expected Asymmetric Return

Expected Downside

Maximum Drawdown

Sector Exposure

Narrative Exposure

Liquidity Exposure

Risk Score

Confidence Score

---

# Position Removal

Sell or reduce a position when:

Investment thesis breaks.

Evidence deteriorates.

Better opportunity appears.

Risk becomes unacceptable.

Market reprices beyond reasonable value.

---

# Final Principle

The purpose of the portfolio is not to own many projects.

The purpose is to allocate capital to the highest quality asymmetric opportunities while protecting long-term capital.
