# SEARCH STRATEGY

## Objective

The objective is NOT to find popular projects.

The objective is to discover statistically significant asymmetric opportunities before the broader market recognizes them.

The search engine must continuously scan the crypto ecosystem for emerging signals.

---

# Search Order

The AI must always follow this order.

## Layer 1 — Market Scan

Scan the entire crypto market.

Include every listed project.

Do not limit the search to the Top 100.

Priority should be given to projects ranked between 100 and 1000.

---

## Layer 2 — Narrative Scan

Identify emerging narratives.

Examples:

AI

DePIN

Physical AI

Machine Economy

Real World Assets

Robotics

Gaming

Privacy

Infrastructure

Data

Oracles

Stablecoins

Payments

Identity

Any newly emerging category

Do not assume current narratives will remain dominant.

---

## Layer 3 — Fundamental Scan

Collect:

Market Cap

FDV

Circulating Supply

Revenue

Fees

Users

TVL

Growth Rate

GitHub Activity

Developer Activity

Wallet Growth

Protocol Revenue

Token Velocity

Burn Rate

Buyback Activity

Inflation

Unlock Schedule

Exchange Listings

---

## Layer 4 — Smart Money Scan

Track:

VC accumulation

Whale accumulation

Exchange inflows

Exchange outflows

Developer wallets

Foundation wallets

Early investor wallets

Dormant wallets becoming active

Insider selling

Large OTC movements

---

## Layer 5 — Historical Pattern Scan

Compare every project with historical winners.

Examples:

Bitcoin

Ethereum

BNB

Chainlink

Aave

Maker

Render

Solana

Injective

Hyperliquid

Bittensor

The objective is to detect repeated patterns before price appreciation.

---

## Layer 6 — Mispricing Scan

Search for evidence that the market is pricing the asset incorrectly.

Possible causes include:

Wrong narrative

Liquidity discount

Temporary fear

VC overhang

Complexity discount

Lack of exchange listings

Negative market sentiment

Hidden revenue

Hidden adoption

Hidden network effects

---

## Layer 7 — Elimination

Reject projects that fail any major criterion.

Examples:

No working product

Fake partnerships

No users

No development

Unsustainable tokenomics

Centralized ownership

Weak security

No competitive advantage

No measurable catalyst

---

## Layer 8 — Ranking

Rank surviving projects.

Ranking must NOT depend on popularity.

Ranking must depend only on evidence.

---

## Continuous Updating

The search process never ends.

Every new piece of information must trigger a re-evaluation.

Every score must remain dynamic.

The system must continuously search for new asymmetric opportunities.
