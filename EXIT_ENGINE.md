# EXIT ENGINE

## Purpose

The Exit Engine determines when and why capital should be reduced or completely removed from an investment.

Buying is only half of investing.

Selling determines realized returns.

Project Hunter must never hold a project simply because it once had a strong thesis.

---

# First Principle

Never sell because of emotions.

Never hold because of hope.

Every exit decision must be based on measurable evidence.

---

# Types of Exit

Hunter recognizes five types of exits.

Full Exit

Partial Exit

Risk Reduction

Profit Protection

Emergency Exit

---

# Reasons for Exit

Exit decisions may be triggered by:

Investment thesis failure

Market repricing

Fundamental deterioration

Risk increase

Better opportunity

Portfolio rebalancing

Macro deterioration

Narrative exhaustion

---

# Thesis Failure

Immediately re-evaluate the position if:

Revenue declines significantly.

Developer activity collapses.

Major customers leave.

Tokenomics worsen.

Smart money distributes.

Competitive advantage disappears.

Management credibility declines.

---

# Valuation Exit

Reduce exposure if:

Market valuation becomes significantly higher than estimated intrinsic value.

Expected future return becomes unattractive.

Market expectations become unrealistic.

---

# Narrative Exit

Reduce exposure when:

The narrative becomes mainstream.

Retail enthusiasm becomes excessive.

Every project claims the same narrative.

Media attention reaches extremes.

---

# Smart Money Exit

Watch for:

Large whale distribution

VC selling

Foundation selling

Treasury liquidation

Exchange inflows

These signals increase exit probability.

---

# Risk Exit

Reduce exposure if:

Risk Score increases materially.

Confidence declines.

Macro deteriorates.

Liquidity weakens.

Competition strengthens.

---

# Opportunity Cost Exit

Capital should migrate toward stronger opportunities.

Ask:

Would I buy this position today?

If not,

why am I still holding it?

---

# Partial Profit Taking

Hunter prefers gradual exits.

Avoid attempting to sell the exact top.

Reduce exposure progressively as evidence weakens.

---

# Emergency Exit

Immediate review required after:

Critical exploit

Fraud

Founder resignation

Major regulatory action

Treasury collapse

Security failure

Permanent thesis destruction

---

# Re-entry

Selling does not permanently reject a project.

Projects may become attractive again if:

Valuation improves.

Fundamentals strengthen.

Risk declines.

Narrative restarts.

Revenue accelerates.

---

# Exit Checklist

Before exiting verify:

Has the thesis changed?

Has valuation changed?

Has probability changed?

Has risk changed?

Has a better opportunity appeared?

Document every decision.

---

# Final Principle

Buying creates opportunity.

Selling realizes results.

The objective is not selling at the exact top.

The objective is maximizing long-term capital growth while protecting against permanent capital loss.
