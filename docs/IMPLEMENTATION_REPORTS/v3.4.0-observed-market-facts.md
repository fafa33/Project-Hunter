## Provider Scope

Initial concrete provider:

- Provider: CoinGecko
- Source ID: `coingecko-coin-market-facts`
- Endpoint family: `https://api.coingecko.com/api/v3/coins/{listing_id}`
- Parser: `coingecko-coin-v1`
- Quote currencies initially registered: USD and EUR
- Historical limitation: current coin endpoint with provider `last_updated` timestamp only

Supported observed facts:

- spot price;
- circulating supply;
- total supply;
- max supply;
- market capitalization;
- fully diluted valuation when explicitly published;
- trading volume.

CoinGecko remains an observed-fact provider only. No provider output is accepted as fair value, intrinsic value, comparative valuation, mispricing, asymmetry, ranking, or recommendation.

## Authority Boundaries

Flow:

```text
Registered Provider
→ ObservedMarketFactService
→ ObservedMarketFactRepository
→ Hunter generic SQL persistence
```

Provider responsibilities:

- HTTPS acquisition;
- response parsing;
- typed success/unavailable outcomes;
- raw payload hashing;
- no persistence or analytical conclusions.

Service responsibilities:

- registry and capability authorization;
- exact provider, endpoint, parser, and registry-fingerprint validation;
- canonical entity, asset, representation, chain/contract, and provider-listing boundaries;
- quote currency and unit validation;
- time and freshness policy;
- fact-specific numeric validation;
- quality and conflict state preservation;
- deterministic logical identity, record identity, and content hashing;
- correction authorization;
- write-plan authorization;
- strict-known read semantics.

Repository responsibilities:

- atomic authorized writes;
- immutable insert-or-identical behavior;
- divergent identity rejection;
- exact record and lineage retrieval;
- strict-known indexed selection;
- operational availability-event storage.

The repository exposes no public `save_record` or provider-facing persistence method. Unauthorized write plans are rejected.

## Identity, Units, and Representation

Every authoritative fact is bound to:

- canonical entity ID;
- canonical asset/claim ID;
- canonical representation ID;
- chain and contract identity where applicable;
- separate provider listing ID.

Provider listing identity cannot substitute for canonical identity. Chain and contract scope must be supplied together. Cross-representation request/result substitution fails closed.

Quoted facts require the authorized quote currency. Supply facts carry no quote currency. Units are source-registry controlled and exact.

## Replay and Lineage

Strict-known selection requires:

- `effective_at <= effective_as_of`;
- `recorded_at <= known_by`;
- explicit `known_at <= known_by`;
- exact entity, representation, fact type, and quote currency;
- accepted quality state;
- no open or contested conflict.

There is no current-state, latest, raw-file, manual-value, Dashboard, or post-cutoff fallback.

Corrections append immutable successor records with:

- preserved logical ID;
- explicit predecessor record ID;
- correction reason;
- new deterministic content and record hashes.

Historical reads before the successor known/recorded cutoff retain the predecessor. Later cutoffs select the eligible successor.

## Unavailable, Stale, and Conflict Behavior

Registry-authorized unavailable outcomes create operational availability events only.

They never create observed facts containing:

- zero;
- null substitutes;
- previous values;
- medians;
- inferred values;
- optimistic defaults.

Facts exceeding the source freshness threshold are preserved with `quality_state=stale` and excluded from strict-known authoritative selection.

Open or contested conflicts are preserved in the immutable record and excluded from authoritative strict-known selection. No averaging occurs.

## Explicit Exclusions Preserved

This implementation does not add or activate:

- fair value or intrinsic value;
- valuation methodology;
- canonical valuation assessment;
- comparative valuation or peer selection;
- mispricing;
- asymmetry or scenario probability;
- Opportunity scoring or ranking;
- Portfolio Intelligence;
- Dashboard calculations;
- general ranking authority;
- Market Validation formula, weights, Timing, committee, report, or scalar-input changes.

The Market Validation inputs `valuation`, `comparative_valuation`, `mispricing`, and `asymmetry` remain explicitly unavailable.
