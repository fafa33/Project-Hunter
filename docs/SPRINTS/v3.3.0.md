## Mission

Implement a tokenomics intelligence foundation that can represent token assets, supply definitions, unlocks, vesting, allocations, holder concentration, address classifications, exchange-flow observations, conflicts, and future sell-pressure evidence without fabricating unavailable data or changing Hunter's canonical scoring and decision behavior.

Hunter must be able to distinguish:

- what is directly observed;
- what is declared by a source;
- what is inferred only as a proxy;
- what is unavailable, stale, ambiguous, contested, corrected, retracted, or superseded;
- what was known by Hunter at a historical cutoff;
- what was reconstructed after the cutoff.

## Business Objective

Hunter should become better at answering:

> Is this token economically investable, and what evidence limits prevent confident conclusions about supply pressure, unlock risk, holder concentration, and sell pressure?

This sprint does not make Hunter more optimistic or pessimistic. It makes tokenomics evidence explicit, replay-safe, and ready for later analysis phases.

## Architectural Invariants

- SQL is authoritative for structured tokenomics state.
- JSON or list fields must not be authoritative for multi-valued allocations, schedules, lineage, classifications, conflicts, or report membership.
- Missing, stale, partial, unavailable, unknown, ambiguous, contested, and proxy states remain explicit.
- Missing tokenomics data is never fabricated, silently substituted, or treated as negative candidate evidence.
- Proxy signals remain visibly distinct from direct observations.
- Proxy signals may not satisfy direct-data requirements unless policy explicitly permits that role.
- Source disagreement is preserved as evidence-quality information, not automatic proof of weakness or fraud.
- Corrections, retractions, and supersessions append lifecycle history rather than overwrite prior claims.
- Strict historical replay uses both effective-time and recorded-time constraints.
- Reconstructed replay is explicitly labeled and never presented as known at cutoff.
- Candidate Registry remains canonical for investable identity.
- Identity/Trust remains authoritative for identity and source trust state.
- Evidence Intelligence claims remain canonical structured truth where applicable.
- Data Sufficiency and Degraded Mode remain separate from scoring and analytical output.
- Tokenomics evidence must not change scoring, ranking, valuation, committee decisions, Opportunity Timing, Market Validation, alerts, recommendations, trades, or canonical runtime behavior unless a later approved sprint explicitly changes those systems.

## Domain Vocabulary

### Evidence Lifecycle and Status

Valid evidence lifecycle/status values:

- `active`
- `corrected`
- `retracted`
- `superseded`
- `contested`
- `unavailable`
- `ambiguous`
- `proxy`

### Report Modes

Valid report modes:

- `known_by_hunter`
- `reconstructed`

`known_by_hunter` reports may use only records where `effective_at <= cutoff_at` and `recorded_at <= cutoff_at`.

`reconstructed` reports may use later-recorded state only when the output is visibly labeled as reconstructed after the cutoff.

### Supply Metrics and Definition States

Supply metrics must be typed and explicit. Supported metrics include:

- circulating supply
- total supply
- maximum supply
- fully diluted supply
- unlocked supply
- locked supply
- vested supply
- burned supply
- staked supply
- treasury supply
- unknown supply metric

Supply-definition state must preserve whether a definition is active, corrected, retracted, superseded, contested, unavailable, ambiguous, or proxy-derived.

### Allocation Categories

Allocation categories must be typed and normalized. Supported categories include:

- community
- ecosystem
- treasury
- team
- investor
- advisor
- foundation
- liquidity
- market maker
- exchange
- staking
- burn
- public sale
- private sale
- airdrop
- rewards
- unknown
- other declared

### Vesting and Unlock States

Vesting schedules, schedule segments, and unlock events must use explicit states for planned, active, completed, delayed, cancelled, corrected, retracted, superseded, contested, unavailable, ambiguous, and proxy-derived records as appropriate.

Fully unlocked supply must not be conflated with low sell-pressure risk.

### Holder and Address Classification Categories

Address classifications must be typed and evidence-backed. Valid identity categories are:

- `treasury`
- `team`
- `investor`
- `market_maker`
- `exchange`
- `bridge`
- `contract`
- `burn`
- `staking`
- `unknown`
- `other_declared`

`whale` must not be an address identity category.

Large balances may support concentration metrics, but they must not create ownership, entity, investor, team, exchange, market-maker, treasury, bridge, contract, or whale attribution by themselves.

A contract role does not prove beneficial ownership of deposited balances.

### Attribution, Verification, Confidence, Availability, and Coverage

Attribution basis must distinguish official disclosures, validated claims, source documents, provider labels, on-chain contract roles, balance-only observations, manual review, unknown attribution, and proxy attribution.

Verification and confidence states must distinguish verified, unverified, disputed, high confidence, medium confidence, low confidence, unknown, contested, unavailable, ambiguous, and proxy states.

Availability and coverage states must distinguish available, stale, partial, unavailable, unknown, ambiguous, contested, gap, thin, estimated, and proxy states where applicable.

### Market Observation and Exchange-Flow Coverage

Venue market observations and exchange-flow windows must represent coverage explicitly. Thin, partial, stale, estimated, unavailable, unknown, and proxy coverage must never be presented as complete direct observation.

### Conflict and Resolution States

Conflict and resolution state must preserve open, contested, resolved, corrected, superseded, rejected, withdrawn, retracted, unavailable, and ambiguous states as appropriate.

Conflicts must preserve every member observation or claim. The system must not silently choose a winner.

## SQL-Authoritative Persistence Scope

Phase A and subsequent phases must preserve normalized SQL-authoritative persistence for:

- token assets and token representations;
- immutable evidence artifacts and atomic evidence claims;
- evidence lifecycle events;
- supply observations and supply-definition reconciliations;
- allocation definitions;
- vesting schedules, schedule segments, and unlock events;
- holder balance snapshots and holder entries;
- address classifications and classification-evidence links;
- venue market observations, transfer observations, and exchange-flow windows;
- observation conflicts, conflict members, and resolution events;
- future report-run and report-observation lineage;
- sufficiency-assessment persistence where appropriate in the existing Data Sufficiency architecture.

Association tables are required for every many-to-many evidence or lineage relationship.

Required constraints and indexes include:

- token representation plus supply metric plus effective and observed time;
- allocation, schedule, and effective range;
- unlock-event time;
- holder snapshot plus address;
- address classification plus category plus validity interval;
- token, venue, and observation window;
- report execution identity and report cutoff;
- lifecycle predecessor chain.

## Evidence and Replay Semantics

Every tokenomics observation must preserve:

- source provenance;
- source authority where available;
- lifecycle status;
- effective time;
- recorded time;
- observed time where applicable;
- historical cutoff semantics;
- confidence and verification state;
- whether it is direct, declared, derived, proxy, unavailable, ambiguous, or contested.

Strict replay must use only state eligible at the cutoff:

- `effective_at <= cutoff_at`
- `recorded_at <= cutoff_at`

Reconstructed replay may use records recorded after the cutoff only when labeled as reconstructed after cutoff.

Corrections, retractions, and supersessions must append lifecycle events and predecessor links. They must not overwrite original claims, raw evidence artifacts, or prior lifecycle history.

## Data Sufficiency and Degraded Mode

Tokenomics reporting and future analytical surfaces must expose Data Sufficiency separately from analytical or scoring output.

Sufficiency assessments must state:

- required data;
- available, stale, partial, unavailable, ambiguous, contested, and proxy states;
- direct observations versus proxy usage;
- source disagreement;
- coverage;
- freshness;
- confidence limits;
- degraded-mode limitations;
- what remains supportable;
- which conclusions are not supportable.

Missing data lowers sufficiency and confidence, not a candidate's underlying score or investment merit.

Strict and reconstructed historical sufficiency assessments must remain cutoff-safe and visibly distinct.

## Provider and Automation Boundaries

This sprint must not introduce paid-provider dependency, live provider collection, RPC calls, scheduler jobs, CLI commands, dashboards, reports, or runtime automation unless explicitly assigned to a later phase.

Provider-dependent jobs must not retry noisily or fabricate empty knowledge when providers are unavailable.

No secrets or API keys may be placed in files, shell history, logs, reports, migrations, fixtures, or documentation.

## Future Reports

Future tokenomics reports must be able to show:

- supply definitions and disagreements;
- current and historical supply observations;
- allocation definitions;
- vesting schedules and unlock events;
- holder balance snapshots and concentration;
- address classifications with evidence;
- exchange-flow coverage;
- conflicts and resolution history;
- sufficiency and degraded-mode limitations;
- strict known-by-Hunter replay;
- reconstructed-after-cutoff replay.

Reports must not present reconstructed records as known at cutoff.

Reports must not transform concentration into ownership attribution without evidence.

Reports must not treat holder concentration, unlocks, exchange deposits, or transfer activity as automatic selling intent.

## Implementation Phases

### Phase A: Contracts, Vocabulary, and Migrations

Implement typed domain contracts, enums, SQL-authoritative normalized migrations, repository interfaces, foreign keys, unique constraints, association tables, and indexes for the persistence scope defined in this sprint.

Phase A must not implement live collection, providers, scheduler jobs, configs, reports, CLI commands, scoring, ranking, valuation, committee behavior, timing, alerts, trades, or recommendations.

Acceptance criteria:

- all required vocabulary exists as typed contracts;
- normalized SQL tables exist for required persistence boundaries;
- multi-valued lineage is represented through relation tables;
- duplicate logical evidence ingestion is idempotent;
- corrections and retractions append lifecycle history;
- conflicting claims coexist;
- balance-only records cannot create owner, entity, or whale attribution;
- required indexes and constraints exist;
- existing tests remain green;
- no runtime side effect is introduced.

### Phase B: Evidence Intake and Normalization

Implement deterministic intake services that normalize declared tokenomics facts, source documents, manually supplied observations, and existing internal evidence into the Phase A contracts.

Phase B must preserve raw evidence artifacts, atomic claims, lifecycle status, source provenance, effective time, recorded time, and historical replay semantics.

It must not perform live provider collection unless separately approved.

### Phase C: Supply, Allocation, Vesting, and Unlock Evaluation

Implement services that evaluate supply definitions, allocation records, vesting schedules, schedule segments, and unlock events from persisted evidence.

Evaluations must distinguish direct observations, declared schedules, derived calculations, unavailable data, stale data, ambiguous records, contested records, and proxy signals.

Fully unlocked supply must not be treated as low sell-pressure risk without evidence.

### Phase D: Holder Concentration and Exchange-Flow Evaluation

Implement services that evaluate holder snapshots, holder concentration, address classifications, transfer observations, venue market observations, and exchange-flow windows.

Large holders must not be treated as imminent sellers without evidence.

Balance-only data must not infer team, investor, exchange, market-maker, treasury, bridge, contract, beneficial owner, or whale identity.

Exchange-flow windows must preserve coverage limits and proxy status.

### Phase E: Conflict Handling, Sufficiency, Reporting, and Operational Integration

Implement cross-source conflict handling, sufficiency/degraded-mode assessment, cutoff-safe report surfaces, and any approved operational integration.

Reports must show tokenomics sufficiency separately from analytical and scoring output.

Automation must remain within existing Scheduler boundaries and must not fabricate evidence or create noisy provider-dependent failures.
