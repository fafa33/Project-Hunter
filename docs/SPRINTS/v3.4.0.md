# Sprint v3.4.0: Canonical Valuation Foundation

## Status

- Sprint: Canonical Valuation Foundation
- Release: `v3.4.0`
- Status: approved for implementation planning
- Architecture source:
  - ADR 0021 — Canonical Valuation Evidence Authority
  - ADR 0016 — Canonical Entity Identity
  - ADR 0020 — Evidence Intelligence Authority
  - Existing Hunter runtime architecture

## Mission

Implement the first production Canonical Valuation Foundation so Hunter can transform observed market evidence into immutable, reproducible, evidence-backed valuation records without introducing analytical ambiguity or modifying the existing runtime architecture.

This sprint establishes the canonical valuation authority required for all future Comparative Valuation, Mispricing Detection, Asymmetry Analysis, Opportunity Assessment, Opportunity Ranking, and Intrinsic Value work.

The implementation must separate observed facts from analytical conclusions and ensure that every valuation record can be reconstructed deterministically from persisted evidence.

---

# Architectural Objectives

The Canonical Valuation Foundation introduces Hunter's first authoritative valuation layer.

Its responsibilities are limited to:

- collecting valuation inputs;
- normalizing observed valuation evidence;
- producing canonical valuation records;
- preserving complete provenance;
- enabling deterministic historical replay;
- supporting future valuation methodologies.

The valuation layer does **not** decide whether an asset is a good investment.

It produces authoritative valuation evidence only.

Investment decisions remain downstream responsibilities.

---

# Architectural Invariants

The following rules are mandatory.

## Authority

- Evidence remains the only source of truth.
- Valuation is derived from evidence.
- AI is never a valuation authority.
- External providers never become valuation authorities.
- SQL remains the authoritative persistence layer.

---

## Separation of Responsibilities

Observed facts and analytical conclusions are separate record families.

Providers may supply:

- prices;
- circulating supply;
- fully diluted supply;
- market capitalization;
- liquidity;
- trading volume;
- protocol revenue;
- fees;
- treasury balances;
- token emissions;
- unlock schedules;
- on-chain observations;
- verified financial metrics.

Providers must never directly supply:

- intrinsic value;
- fair value;
- undervaluation;
- overvaluation;
- opportunity scores;
- ranking scores;
- investment recommendations;
- asymmetry conclusions.

Those outputs belong exclusively to Hunter.

---

## Canonical Ownership

Hunter exclusively owns:

- valuation methodology;
- normalization;
- evidence selection;
- confidence projection;
- valuation lineage;
- valuation lifecycle;
- historical reconstruction;
- valuation corrections;
- valuation supersession.

External providers may contribute observations only.

---

## Historical Replay

Historical replay remains strict.

A valuation is considered known only when:

- its supporting evidence was effective at the requested cutoff; and
- the evidence had already been recorded by Hunter.

Replay must never use information discovered after the requested historical point.

Reconstruction mode must always remain explicitly identified.

---

## Persistence

Valuation records are immutable.

Corrections never overwrite previous records.

Lifecycle events are append-only.

Current state is always reconstructed from immutable history.

No destructive updates are permitted.

---

## Backward Compatibility

This sprint must not modify:

- Discovery;
- Candidate Registry;
- Identity & Trust;
- Evidence Intelligence;
- Tokenomics;
- Macro Intelligence;
- On-Chain Intelligence;
- Market Validation;
- Opportunity Engine;
- Dashboard;

- existing CLI behavior.

Only additive implementation is permitted.

## Valuation Scope

Canonical valuation is performed against a canonical asset representation rather than an abstract asset identity.

Every valuation shall explicitly identify:

- canonical entity;
- canonical representation;
- valuation horizon;
- valuation methodology;
- valuation family.

Different representations of the same underlying asset may produce independent canonical valuation records.

No valuation may implicitly merge multiple representations.

# Design Principles

The Canonical Valuation Foundation must satisfy the following principles.

1. Deterministic.

The same evidence must always produce the same valuation.

2. Explainable.

Every valuation must expose complete provenance.

3. Replay-safe.

Historical replay must never leak future information.

4. Evidence-first.

Missing evidence must never be replaced by assumptions.

5. Provider-independent.

Replacing a provider must not change Hunter's valuation model.

6. Methodology-versioned.

Future valuation methodologies must coexist without rewriting historical results.

7. SQL-authoritative.

All canonical valuation records must be reconstructable from SQL persistence.

8. Fully auditable.

Every valuation decision must expose its supporting evidence, methodology version, confidence projection, lifecycle history, and correction chain.

# Canonical Domain Model

The valuation domain is composed of independent immutable record families.

Each family owns a single responsibility.

No record may duplicate another record's authority.

---

## ValuationMethodology

Represents a versioned valuation methodology owned exclusively by Hunter.

A methodology defines:

- supported valuation family;
- mathematical model;
- required evidence;
- optional evidence;
- normalization rules;
- confidence rules;
- assumptions;
- output schema;
- methodology version.

Methodologies are immutable.

A methodology revision creates a new methodology version.

Historical valuation records always reference the methodology version used at calculation time.

---

## ValuationInput

Represents one normalized observed input.

Examples include:

- market price;
- circulating supply;
- fully diluted supply;
- realized capitalization;
- protocol revenue;
- annualized fees;
- treasury balance;
- token emissions;
- unlock percentage;
- active addresses;
- transaction volume;
- staking ratio;
- inflation rate.

Valuation inputs are observations.

They never contain analytical conclusions.

---

## ValuationEvidence

Represents the exact evidence supporting a valuation.

Evidence may originate from:

- Evidence Intelligence;
- Tokenomics;
- On-Chain Intelligence;
- Macro Intelligence;
- Market Data;
- Official protocol disclosures;
- Registry-approved providers.

Evidence remains immutable.

Valuation records reference evidence.

Evidence never references valuation.

---

## CanonicalValuation

Represents Hunter's authoritative canonical valuation record family.

Comparative Valuation, Mispricing, and Asymmetry belong to independent authoritative record families introduced by later architectural layers.

A valuation contains:

- candidate identity;
- valuation methodology;
- methodology version;
- valuation timestamp;
- valuation horizon;
- normalized inputs;
- calculated outputs;
- confidence projection;
- evidence references;
- provenance metadata.

CanonicalValuation is the only authoritative valuation record.

No alternative valuation record family may exist.

---

## ValuationLifecycleEvent

Represents append-only lifecycle events.

Examples include:

- created;
- superseded;
- corrected;
- invalidated;
- deprecated;
- unavailable.

Lifecycle history is immutable.

Current state is reconstructed from lifecycle events.

---

## ValuationConfidence

Confidence is not provider supplied.

Hunter calculates confidence using deterministic rules.

Confidence considers only supported factors defined by methodology.

Confidence never represents investment probability.

---

## ValuationCorrection

Corrections never overwrite historical valuation.

Corrections append new records.

The original valuation remains permanently preserved.

Correction lineage must always remain reconstructable.

---

## ValuationLineage

Every valuation must expose complete lineage.

Lineage includes:

- methodology version;
- evidence identifiers;
- normalization version;
- calculation version;
- confidence version;
- lifecycle history;
- correction chain.

Lineage is SQL-authoritative.

Historical lineage must always be replayable without ambiguity.

# Persistence Model

The Canonical Valuation Foundation introduces the first authoritative persistence layer for valuation.

Persistence must satisfy the following properties:

- immutable;
- append-only;
- replay-safe;
- SQL-authoritative;
- deterministic;
- fully auditable.

No valuation information may exist only inside JSON payloads.

Every canonical relationship must be represented through normalized SQL tables.

CanonicalValuation owns authority for canonical valuation only.

## ValuationObservationLink

## ValuationMethodologyDependency

# SQL Requirements

- deterministic writes;

# Identity Rules

Every valuation references exactly one canonical candidate identity.

Valuation records must never reference:

- aliases;
- temporary identifiers;
- provider identifiers;
- ticker symbols.

Identity authority belongs exclusively to the Identity Layer.

---

# Temporal Semantics

Every persisted valuation record must distinguish:

- effective_at
- recorded_at

effective_at represents when the valuation became true.

recorded_at represents when Hunter first knew the valuation.

Historical replay requires both timestamps.

---

# Idempotency

Repeated execution of the same valuation calculation must never create duplicate canonical valuations.

Equivalent calculations must resolve to the existing canonical record.

Only materially different valuation outputs may create new valuation records.

---

# Versioning

The following objects require explicit versioning:

- methodology;
- normalization;
- confidence projection;
- calculation engine;
- valuation schema.

Historical records permanently preserve the versions used during calculation.

---

# Repository Contracts

Repositories own persistence only.

Repositories must never:

- perform valuation calculations;
- normalize observations;
- estimate confidence;
- choose methodologies;
- interpret provider responses.

Repositories expose deterministic persistence interfaces only.

---

# Transaction Boundaries

The following operations must be atomic.

- valuation creation;
- evidence linking;
- observation linking;
- lifecycle append;
- correction append;
- lineage persistence.

Partial valuation persistence is prohibited.

Either the complete valuation exists or no valuation exists.

---

# Historical Reconstruction

The persistence model must support deterministic reconstruction of:

- valuation state;
- methodology;
- evidence;
- observations;
- confidence;
- lifecycle;
- correction lineage.

Historical replay must never require external providers.

Replay depends exclusively on persisted canonical records.

---

## Bitemporal Semantics

Canonical valuation records are bitemporal.

Every record shall preserve both:

- effective time (when the valuation became valid);
- recorded time (when Hunter learned the valuation).

Historical replay must always distinguish:

- known at replay cutoff;
- effective at replay cutoff;
- recorded after replay cutoff.

Replay shall never substitute future knowledge for historical knowledge.

---
