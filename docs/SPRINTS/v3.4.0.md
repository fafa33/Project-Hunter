# Sprint v3.4.0: Canonical Observed Market Facts Foundation

## Status

- Release: `v3.4.0`
- Status: approved implementation specification
- Scope: additive production foundation only
- Primary authority:
  - ADR 0021 — Canonical Valuation Evidence Authority
  - ADR 0020 — Canonical Market Validation Input Authority and Strict-Known Replay
  - ADR 0016 — Runtime Analytical Authority
  - ADR 0009 — Repository Purification
  - ADR 0005 — Entity Model Separation

## Mission

Implement the first production foundation required by ADR 0021: immutable, identity-bound, strict-known, provider-observed market facts.

This sprint does **not** implement fair value, comparative valuation, mispricing, asymmetry, Opportunity scoring, ranking, or investment recommendations.

Its sole analytical purpose is to preserve trustworthy observed market facts that later valuation services may consume by exact record ID and version.

## Authority Boundary

The production flow is:

```text
Registered Provider
→ ObservedMarketFactService
→ ObservedMarketFactRepository
→ Dedicated SQL Persistence
```

Providers own acquisition and normalization of facts they actually publish.

`ObservedMarketFactService` solely owns:

- source and capability authorization;
- canonical entity and representation validation;
- units and quote-currency validation;
- effective, recorded, and known-time policy;
- provenance validation;
- conflict and quality-state decisions;
- deterministic identity;
- correction and supersession authorization;
- persistence plans;
- strict-known replay selection.

Repositories own only deterministic storage and retrieval of service-authorized records.

No provider, repository, scheduler, Dashboard, report, test, configuration file, or field name becomes an analytical or valuation authority.

## Required Record Family

### `ObservedMarketFactRecord`

Every record must contain at least:

- `record_id`;
- `logical_id`;
- `schema_version`;
- `semantic_version`;
- canonical entity ID;
- canonical asset/claim ID;
- canonical representation ID;
- chain and contract identity where applicable;
- provider listing ID;
- fact type;
- numeric value;
- exact unit;
- quote currency where applicable;
- venue or aggregation scope;
- observation/effective time;
- recorded time;
- explicit known time;
- provider and registry-source identity;
- provider/parser/source versions;
- raw payload hash;
- evidence or acquisition record references;
- quality flags;
- conflict state;
- confidence state where supported;
- correction/supersession lineage;
- canonical content hash.

Records are immutable and append-only.

A correction creates a successor record. It never mutates or deletes the predecessor.

## Supported Fact Types

The first implementation may support only facts explicitly observed by approved providers:

- spot price;
- circulating supply;
- total supply;
- max supply;
- market capitalization;
- fully diluted valuation only when the provider publishes the underlying observed value and scope explicitly;
- trading volume;
- provider/venue aggregation metadata.

Additional fact types require explicit registry capability and semantic validation.

## Explicitly Prohibited Semantics

The following must not be emitted or inferred in this sprint:

- fair value;
- intrinsic value;
- valuation score;
- comparative valuation;
- peer selection;
- mispricing;
- undervaluation or overvaluation;
- asymmetry;
- scenario probability;
- opportunity score;
- ranking;
- recommendation;
- value-capture conclusion;
- inferred revenue attribution;
- inferred token entitlement;
- normalized Market Validation scalar inputs.

Provider categories, metadata completeness, market-cap rank, popularity, labels, or provider-generated targets are not valuation evidence.

## Identity and Representation Rules

Every persisted fact must bind to the exact canonical economic scope.

The service must distinguish:

- project;
- protocol;
- network;
- native asset;
- token;
- contract;
- wrapped representation;
- bridged representation;
- provider listing.

A provider listing is never treated as the canonical economic entity.

Facts for different representations must not be merged implicitly.

Ambiguous, unresolved, mismatched, or cross-representation records fail closed and are not persisted as authoritative facts.

## Time and Replay Rules

Strict-known replay is mandatory.

A fact is eligible at a requested cutoff only when:

- `effective_at <= effective_as_of`;
- `recorded_at <= known_by`;
- explicit `known_at <= known_by`;
- the record is compatible with the requested entity, representation, schema, source, and fact type;
- the selected lineage was valid at the requested cutoff.

There is no:

- `latest` fallback;
- current-state fallback;
- timestamp backfill;
- post-cutoff substitution;
- unknown-known-time promotion;
- raw-file fallback;
- Dashboard fallback;
- manual value fallback.

Unknown, unavailable, stale, conflicted, incompatible, or insufficient facts remain explicitly unavailable.

## Source Registry

The implementation must use an explicit source registry.

Each source entry must declare:

- provider identity;
- approved host and endpoint pattern;
- capability allow-list;
- parser version;
- supported fact types;
- supported entity and representation scope;
- units and quote currencies;
- freshness policy;
- historical support and limitations;
- required provenance fields;
- enabled/disabled state.

An unregistered, disabled, capability-mismatched, host-mismatched, endpoint-mismatched, parser-mismatched, or scope-mismatched result must be rejected before persistence.

CoinGecko may be used only as a provider of observed market facts actually published by its approved endpoint. It must not supply analytical conclusions.

## Persistence Requirements

Use a dedicated SQL-authoritative boundary for observed market facts.

Persistence must provide:

- immutable insert-or-identical semantics;
- rejection of divergent duplicate identities;
- service-authorized writes only;
- deterministic canonical hashes;
- exact record-ID and version reads;
- strict-known indexed selection;
- correction and supersession lineage;
- transactionally atomic record and lineage persistence;
- idempotent retries;
- migration versioning;
- backup-compatible normalized schema.

Repository APIs must not:

- create timestamps;
- resolve identity;
- select source authority;
- normalize unknown units;
- infer missingness;
- resolve conflicts;
- create corrections;
- select current/latest state as replay authority.

## Acquisition and Service Behavior

The provider adapter must return typed acquisition outcomes, including successful and unavailable results.

Unavailable outcomes may be persisted only as operational availability evidence when:

- the source and capability were registry-approved;
- the request identity and scope were authorized;
- the outcome preserves provider, endpoint, parser, request, time, and failure provenance.

Unavailable outcomes must never create an `ObservedMarketFactRecord` with a zero, null substitute, previous value, median, or optimistic default.

The service must validate every successful result before persistence.

## First Provider Scope

The initial concrete provider scope is deliberately narrow:

- provider: CoinGecko;
- endpoint family: approved coin market-data endpoint already used by Hunter;
- quote currency: explicitly declared by the request and response;
- facts: price, supply measures, market capitalization, fully diluted valuation where published, and volume;
- target: registry-resolved canonical asset representation and provider listing.

No provider expansion is required for sprint completion.

## Package and File Direction

Implementation should add or extend a dedicated market-facts boundary under `src/hunter/` without placing domain policy in generic providers or repositories.

Expected responsibilities:

```text
models.py       immutable record and typed outcome contracts
registry.py     source/capability configuration validation
providers.py    acquisition adapters only
service.py      authority, identity, time, conflict, lineage, write plans
repository.py   SQL persistence and strict-known reads only
configuration.py source and persistence configuration
```

Exact filenames may follow existing repository conventions, but responsibility boundaries are mandatory.

## CLI and Automation

A minimal operational CLI command may be added for explicit acquisition and status reporting.

CLI and Scheduler may invoke the service but must not:

- calculate valuation;
- select unsupported sources;
- bypass registry validation;
- write directly to repositories;
- reinterpret missing facts;
- expose experimental or analytical conclusions.

Automation is optional for sprint completion unless an existing approved job framework can invoke the service additively without changing analytical authority.

## Market Validation Boundary

This sprint must not activate or populate the Market Validation inputs:

- `valuation`;
- `comparative_valuation`;
- `mispricing`;
- `asymmetry`.

They remain explicitly unavailable under ADR 0021.

Observed market facts are prerequisites only. Their existence does not authorize a scalar score or remove missingness.

## Out of Scope

The following are prohibited in `v3.4.0`:

- `FundamentalEvidenceRecord`;
- `ValueCaptureRuleSnapshot`;
- `SupplyBasisSnapshot` beyond provider-observed supply facts;
- `ValuationMethodologySnapshot`;
- `FairValueEstimateRecord`;
- `ValuationAssessmentRecord`;
- peer-universe policy or snapshots;
- comparative valuation;
- mispricing calculations;
- scenario sets or probabilities;
- asymmetry calculations;
- historical calibration or scalar normalization;
- Opportunity integration;
- ranking or Portfolio Intelligence;
- Dashboard-side calculations;
- changes to Market Validation weights, formulas, ranking, Timing, committee fields, or reports;
- migration or promotion of experimental Opportunity, Probability, Pattern, Necessity, Fusion, or Committee outputs.

## Required Tests

The implementation must include deterministic tests proving at least:

### Authority and Registry

- unregistered source rejection;
- disabled source rejection;
- capability mismatch rejection;
- provider/host/endpoint/parser mismatch rejection;
- forged successful result rejection;
- forged unavailable result rejection;
- provider cannot persist directly;
- repository direct authoritative mutation is rejected or inaccessible.

### Identity and Units

- canonical entity and representation linkage is required;
- provider listing cannot substitute for canonical identity;
- cross-representation facts are rejected;
- quote-currency mismatch is rejected;
- unsupported or ambiguous units are rejected;
- zero and negative values follow fact-specific validation rules rather than generic acceptance.

### Persistence and Lineage

- insert-or-identical retry is idempotent;
- divergent duplicate identity is rejected;
- corrections append successors;
- predecessors remain readable;
- atomic write failure leaves no partial authoritative state;
- exact record/version retrieval is deterministic.

### Replay

- strict-known selection excludes post-cutoff records;
- effective-time cutoff is enforced;
- recorded-time cutoff is enforced;
- explicit known-time cutoff is enforced;
- unknown known time cannot satisfy strict replay;
- no `latest` fallback occurs;
- correction lineage is selected correctly at historical cutoffs.

### Missingness and Conflicts

- provider failure does not create zero-valued facts;
- stale data remains unavailable where policy requires;
- conflicts are preserved rather than silently averaged;
- missing facts do not activate valuation-family Market Validation inputs.

### Compatibility

- existing Market Validation outputs and weights remain unchanged;
- canonical Timing remains unchanged;
- existing provider, automation, Dashboard, and operational behavior remains compatible;
- full quality gates pass.

## Completion Criteria

Sprint `v3.4.0` is complete only when:

- one dedicated service-owned observed-market-fact authority exists;
- the initial CoinGecko fact scope is registry-gated;
- immutable SQL persistence and exact lineage exist;
- strict-known replay is proven;
- identity and representation boundaries are enforced;
- unavailable and conflicting states fail closed;
- no valuation or Opportunity conclusion is produced;
- all four valuation-family Market Validation inputs remain unavailable;
- no existing production authority is changed;
- focused and complete quality gates pass;
- independent architecture review reports no unresolved blocker.
