# Sprint v3.5.0: Supply Basis and Value-Capture Evidence Foundation

## Status

- Release: `v3.5.0`
- Status: approved implementation specification
- Scope: additive production foundation only
- Primary authority:
  - ADR 0021 — Canonical Valuation Evidence Authority
  - ADR 0020 — Canonical Market Validation Input Authority and Strict-Known Replay
  - ADR 0016 — Runtime Analytical Authority
  - ADR 0009 — Repository Purification
  - ADR 0005 — Entity Model Separation
  - Sprint v3.4.0 — Canonical Observed Market Facts Foundation

## Mission

Implement the second production foundation required by ADR 0021: immutable, evidence-backed supply-basis and value-capture records that establish what economic claim is being valued and how value may legally or mechanically accrue to that claim.

This sprint does not implement fair value, comparative valuation, mispricing, asymmetry, Opportunity scoring, ranking, Portfolio Intelligence, or investment recommendations.

Its sole analytical purpose is to make future valuation inputs semantically defensible by preserving:

- the exact supply basis used for a claim;
- the relationship between project, protocol, network, asset, token, contract, and representation;
- value-capture mechanisms actually supported by evidence;
- dilution, emission, unlock, burn, buyback, staking, distribution, and entitlement rules;
- explicit unavailable, conflicted, and unsupported states.

## Authority Boundary

The production flow is:

```text
Registered Evidence Source
→ SupplyAndValueCaptureService
→ SupplyAndValueCaptureRepository
→ Dedicated SQL-Authoritative Persistence
```

Providers and adapters own acquisition and parsing only.

`SupplyAndValueCaptureService` solely owns:

- source and capability authorization;
- canonical entity, claim, asset, token, and representation validation;
- distinction between observed facts and analytical interpretation;
- supply-basis classification;
- value-capture rule classification;
- evidence sufficiency decisions;
- unit, denominator, horizon, and scope validation;
- effective, recorded, and known-time policy;
- conflict and missingness decisions;
- deterministic identity;
- correction and supersession authorization;
- persistence write plans;
- strict-known replay selection.

Repositories own deterministic storage and exact retrieval only.

No provider, repository, scheduler, Dashboard, report, test, configuration file, or field name becomes valuation authority.

## Required Record Families

### `SupplyBasisSnapshot`

Every snapshot must contain at least:

- `record_id`;
- `logical_id`;
- schema and semantic versions;
- canonical entity ID;
- canonical economic claim ID;
- canonical asset ID;
- canonical representation ID;
- chain and contract identity where applicable;
- supply-basis type;
- quantity;
- exact unit;
- denominator meaning;
- effective time;
- recorded time;
- explicit known time;
- source and parser identity;
- evidence references;
- raw payload or disclosure hash;
- quality state;
- conflict state;
- correction/supersession lineage;
- canonical content hash.

Supported supply-basis types may include only explicitly evidenced categories:

- circulating supply;
- total supply;
- max supply;
- fully diluted supply;
- issued supply;
- treasury-held supply;
- locked supply;
- vested-but-unreleased supply;
- burned supply;
- protocol-owned supply;
- other explicitly registered categories.

Categories must not be inferred from labels alone.

### `ValueCaptureRuleSnapshot`

Every rule snapshot must contain at least:

- `record_id`;
- `logical_id`;
- schema and semantic versions;
- canonical entity ID;
- canonical economic claim ID;
- canonical asset/token ID;
- canonical representation ID;
- rule type;
- entitlement scope;
- beneficiary scope;
- source economic flow;
- destination economic flow;
- trigger or condition;
- distribution formula where explicitly documented;
- rate or proportion where explicitly documented;
- governance or contract authority;
- effective, recorded, and known times;
- source, parser, evidence, and raw-hash references;
- quality and conflict states;
- correction/supersession lineage;
- canonical content hash.

Supported value-capture rule types may include only explicitly evidenced mechanisms:

- burn;
- buyback;
- buyback-and-burn;
- staking distribution;
- fee distribution;
- revenue distribution;
- protocol-owned liquidity accrual;
- redemption entitlement;
- collateral entitlement;
- governance-directed distribution;
- no direct value capture;
- unavailable or unsupported.

A provider or project claim such as “revenue generating,” “deflationary,” or “utility token” is insufficient by itself.

### `FundamentalEvidenceRecord`

This record preserves the evidence used to support a supply or value-capture conclusion.

It must contain at least:

- canonical identity scope;
- evidence type;
- source authority tier;
- exact source reference;
- parser and schema versions;
- effective, recorded, and known times;
- extracted claim;
- exact units and amounts when present;
- raw content hash;
- quality, conflict, and missingness state;
- correction/supersession lineage.

Fundamental evidence does not itself become a valuation score.

## Source Authority

The implementation must use an explicit source registry.

Permitted source classes may include:

- official protocol or foundation disclosures;
- verified token contracts and on-chain observations;
- audited financial or treasury disclosures;
- governance proposals and enacted votes;
- official staking, burn, buyback, distribution, or redemption documentation;
- approved public aggregators for observed facts only.

Each registry entry must declare:

- source identity;
- authority tier;
- approved host and endpoint or document pattern;
- parser version;
- allowed capabilities;
- supported entity and representation scope;
- supported units and categories;
- freshness and historical limits;
- required provenance fields;
- enabled/disabled state.

Unregistered, disabled, capability-mismatched, host-mismatched, endpoint-mismatched, parser-mismatched, or scope-mismatched outcomes must be rejected before authoritative persistence.

## Supply-Basis Rules

The service must distinguish observed quantity from analytical denominator choice.

Examples:

- CoinGecko circulating supply is an observed market fact, not automatically the canonical valuation denominator.
- Contract `totalSupply` is an observed on-chain fact, not automatically circulating supply.
- Max supply is not interchangeable with total supply.
- Locked, treasury, vested, bridged, wrapped, burned, or protocol-owned quantities must not be silently merged.
- Cross-chain and wrapped representations must remain separate unless an explicit canonical aggregation policy exists.

Every snapshot must identify exactly what the number means and what it excludes.

Unknown or conflicting supply basis remains unavailable.

## Value-Capture Rules

A value-capture rule is authoritative only when the service can prove:

- the economic claim that benefits;
- the mechanism by which value moves;
- the trigger or policy;
- the governing contract, proposal, or official rule;
- the applicable representation;
- the effective period;
- the evidence known by the replay cutoff.

The service must reject:

- implied value capture from TVL alone;
- implied value capture from protocol revenue alone;
- marketing claims without enforceable or documented mechanism;
- governance possibilities that have not been enacted;
- temporary campaigns presented as permanent economics;
- staking yield sourced only from inflation without explicit classification;
- buyback claims without evidence of authorization and execution scope;
- burn claims that refer only to inaccessible addresses without verified policy;
- cross-token or cross-entity attribution without explicit entitlement.

## Time and Replay Rules

Strict-known replay is mandatory.

A record is eligible only when:

- `effective_at <= effective_as_of`;
- `recorded_at <= known_by`;
- explicit `known_at <= known_by`;
- the record matches the requested entity, claim, asset, representation, category, schema, and source scope;
- the selected lineage was valid at the requested cutoff.

There is no:

- latest fallback;
- current-state fallback;
- post-cutoff substitution;
- unknown-known-time promotion;
- manual-value fallback;
- Dashboard fallback;
- optimistic interpretation;
- automatic aggregation across representations.

## Persistence Requirements

Use dedicated SQL-authoritative persistence for the new record families.

Persistence must provide:

- immutable insert-or-identical semantics;
- divergent duplicate rejection;
- service-authorized writes only;
- deterministic canonical hashes;
- exact record and version reads;
- strict-known indexed selection;
- correction and supersession lineage;
- atomic record and lineage persistence;
- idempotent retries;
- migration versioning;
- normalized backup-compatible schema.

Repository APIs must not:

- create timestamps;
- infer categories;
- resolve identity;
- select source authority;
- infer value capture;
- select a valuation denominator;
- merge conflicting evidence;
- create corrections;
- use current/latest state as replay authority.

## Initial Concrete Scope

The first implementation must remain deliberately narrow.

Required concrete capabilities:

- ingest provider-observed supply facts from v3.4.0 by exact record ID;
- support one registry-approved official disclosure source for tokenomics or value-capture rules;
- support one registry-approved on-chain source where an exact contract observation is required;
- persist evidence-backed supply-basis snapshots;
- persist evidence-backed value-capture rule snapshots;
- preserve explicit unavailable and conflict states.

No broad provider expansion is required.

## Required Tests

### Authority and Registry

- unregistered source rejection;
- disabled source rejection;
- capability mismatch rejection;
- provider/host/endpoint/parser mismatch rejection;
- forged successful and unavailable result rejection;
- no provider direct persistence;
- no repository direct authoritative mutation.

### Identity and Representation

- canonical entity, claim, asset, and representation are required;
- provider listing cannot substitute for canonical identity;
- wrapped and bridged representations remain distinct;
- cross-entity and cross-token attribution is rejected;
- chain and contract mismatch is rejected.

### Supply Basis

- circulating, total, max, locked, treasury, vested, and burned categories remain distinct;
- totalSupply cannot silently satisfy circulating supply;
- unknown denominator remains unavailable;
- unsupported aggregation is rejected;
- incompatible units are rejected;
- zero and negative values follow category-specific validation.

### Value Capture

- revenue without entitlement does not create value capture;
- TVL without entitlement does not create value capture;
- enacted burn or distribution rule persists with evidence;
- unenacted governance proposal remains non-authoritative;
- temporary campaign does not become permanent rule;
- inflationary staking rewards are classified explicitly;
- no-direct-value-capture state can be represented without becoming a negative score.

### Persistence and Lineage

- insert-or-identical retry is idempotent;
- divergent duplicate identity is rejected;
- corrections append successors;
- predecessors remain readable;
- atomic write failure leaves no partial state;
- exact record/version retrieval is deterministic.

### Replay

- post-cutoff records are excluded;
- effective, recorded, and explicit known-time cutoffs are enforced;
- unknown known time cannot satisfy strict replay;
- no latest fallback occurs;
- historical correction lineage is selected correctly.

### Compatibility

- v3.4.0 observed-market-fact behavior remains unchanged;
- Market Validation outputs and weights remain unchanged;
- canonical Timing remains unchanged;
- Opportunity and ranking remain unchanged;
- all four valuation-family inputs remain unavailable;
- full existing suite remains passing.
