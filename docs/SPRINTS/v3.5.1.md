## Mission

Bring Hunter's existing fundamental-evidence, supply-basis, and value-capture
foundation into full compliance with ADR 0021 and the canonical generic SQL
authority established by the observed-market-facts foundation.

The sprint preserves truthful missingness. It does not estimate fair value and
does not activate any valuation-family Market Validation input.

## Business Objective

Make future valuation work depend on durable, replayable proof of:

- which economic claim is being valued;
- which representation and supply denominator apply;
- whether and how an economic flow is attributable to that claim; and
- what evidence, uncertainty, conflict, and correction history support the
  conclusion.

## Current-State Finding

The existing `hunter.value_capture` package contains useful typed contracts and
service-boundary protections, but its default persistence path is
`data/value_capture/runtime/value_capture.sqlite` and it owns a standalone
SQLite schema. That conflicts with Issue #95 and the post-Issue-#88 canonical
rule that these records must use Hunter's generic SQL authority in
`data/data_ops.sqlite`.

Existing records also require contract strengthening for accounting periods,
attribution references, evidence uncertainty, confidence decomposition,
limitations, supply-policy identity, coherent multi-quantity supply snapshots,
and explicit source-record versions.

## Scope

### Contract remediation

- Preserve and strengthen immutable `FundamentalEvidenceRecord`,
  `ValueCaptureRuleSnapshot`, and `SupplyBasisSnapshot`.
- Add explicit accounting-period, attribution, methodology, source-version,
  confidence, uncertainty, limitations, policy-version, and quality fields
  required by ADR 0021.
- Validate finite decimals, compatible units, complete identity, UTC
  chronology, hashes, confidence bounds, correction lineage, and
  representation/chain consistency.
- Require an explicit value-capture pathway before descriptive fees, revenue,
  TVL, activity, utility, burn, buyback, or distribution observations can
  support valuation evidence.
- Validate supply quantities as one coherent snapshot and prohibit silent
  merging across representations.

### Service authority

- Keep validation, identity checks, conflict decisions, corrections, and write
  authorization inside the canonical service.
- Preserve an unforgeable service-to-repository write capability.
- Distinguish insert-identical retries from divergent duplicate attempts.
- Return explicit rejection or unavailability for ambiguous, stale, disputed,
  unsupported, unit-incompatible, or insufficient inputs.
- Never synthesize zero, median, previous, current/latest, neutral, or
  optimistic values.

### Persistence remediation

- Move the three record families and their acquisition receipts to domain
  snapshots in Hunter's generic SQL persistence authority.
- Use `data/data_ops.sqlite` as the only default durable database.
- Remove runtime dependence on the standalone value-capture SQLite schema.
- Preserve exact IDs, versions, source references, canonical hashes, and
  append-only correction lineage.
- Provide deterministic exact-record, logical-history, strict-known, and
  unresolved-conflict reads.
- Preserve idempotent insert-or-identical behavior and reject divergent reuse.

### Compatibility

- Migrate tests and public construction paths without silently reinterpreting
  persisted legacy data.
- Treat the old standalone store as non-authoritative legacy input; it may be
  imported only by a separately validated migration path.
- Keep existing canonical observed-market-fact authority unchanged.

## Out of Scope

- Valuation methodology selection.
- `ValuationMethodologySnapshot`.
- Fair-value models or `FairValueEstimateRecord`.
- Historical calibration or scalar normalization.
- Peer selection and comparative valuation.
- Mispricing.
- Scenarios, probabilities, payoff estimates, or asymmetry.
- Activation of `valuation`, `comparative_valuation`, `mispricing`, or
  `asymmetry`.
- Dashboard, report, automation, scheduler, or provider authority expansion.
- A universal crypto valuation formula.

## Engineering Principles

- Specification and authority before implementation.
- Exact entity, asset-claim, token, representation, chain, and contract scope.
- Decimal authority without binary-float semantics.
- Immutable, idempotent, append-only records.
- Explicit known time and strict-known replay.
- Complete provenance and exact source versions.
- Conflicts remain visible; no averaging or silent winner selection.
- Reuse the generic SQL repository rather than creating another database.
- Fail closed when evidence, attribution, or compatibility is incomplete.

## Performance

- Exact-record lookup must use deterministic identity.
- Strict-known selection must remain indexable through generic snapshot
  metadata and bounded domain filtering.
- Logical histories must use stable ordering by effective, recorded, known
  times, then record ID.
- Avoid unbounded cross-entity scans and pairwise conflict comparison.
- No bulk provider expansion or automatic backfill is authorized.

## Automation

No new scheduler or automated acquisition job is authorized.

Retries may repeat an identical service-authorized write and must remain
idempotent. Failed validation or persistence must not leave partial receipts,
records, or correction lineage.

## Testing

Tests must cover:

- valid attributable fundamental evidence;
- rejection of descriptive evidence without an explicit value-capture rule;
- evidence-type, unit, decimal, accounting-window, chronology, confidence,
  uncertainty, hash, provenance, and identity validation;
- valid value-capture pathways and preserved limitations;
- unsupported or missing pathways;
- coherent and incoherent supply-basis quantities;
- exact duplicate idempotency and divergent duplicate rejection;
- correction append, predecessor preservation, and no branching lineage;
- strict-known replay before and after a future-known correction;
- multiple-source conflict preservation without averaging or implicit
  resolution;
- service-authorized writes and repository-bypass rejection;
- persistence only in the generic SQL authority;
- continued unavailability of all four valuation-family inputs.

Required gates on one exact final branch HEAD:

```bash
ruff check .
black --check src tests config alembic
mypy
pytest
```

## Acceptance Criteria

1. All three record families meet the ADR 0021 minimum contract.
2. The canonical service is the sole public write authority.
3. Default persistence is `data/data_ops.sqlite` through generic SQL snapshots.
4. No authoritative runtime creates or depends on a standalone
   value-capture database.
5. Insert-identical, divergent duplicate, correction, conflict, and
   strict-known behavior are deterministic.
6. Exact identity, units, provenance, confidence, uncertainty, limitations,
   and source versions survive persistence and replay.
7. Unsupported attribution and incoherent supply remain explicitly
   unavailable or rejected.
8. The four valuation-family inputs remain absent.
9. All required quality gates pass on the final branch HEAD.

## Success Metrics

- Zero authoritative writes outside the generic SQL database.
- Zero repository-bypass paths.
- Zero accepted evidence without exact identity, provenance, and known time.
- Zero implicit conflict resolution or fallback values.
- One hundred percent deterministic replay for supported strict-known cases.
- All valuation-family inputs remain truthfully unavailable.
