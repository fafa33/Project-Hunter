## Mission

Implement Competitive and Peer Intelligence as an additive layer that helps Hunter compare candidates against evidence-backed competitors, substitutes, and algorithmically similar peers without changing canonical scoring or runtime behavior.

This sprint uses Candidate Registry identity, Identity/Trust outcomes, and v3.0.0 Evidence Intelligence canonical claims as inputs. It must improve candidate selection by making competitive context, peer-set quality, substitution risk, differentiation, and missing competitive evidence visible.

## Business Objective

Hunter should become better at answering:

> Among discovered and trusted candidates, which deserve deeper research because their competitive position appears strong, weak, unclear, or under-evidenced?

The release must reduce false conviction caused by analyzing a candidate in isolation.

## Architectural Invariants

- Candidate Registry remains the canonical investable identity boundary.
- Identity and Trust must be consulted before accepting candidate links.
- Evidence Intelligence `KnowledgeClaim` remains the canonical structured truth source.
- AI output may propose evidence but never becomes truth unless validated through v3.0.0 evidence validation.
- SQL remains authoritative; graph-ready projections may be consumed, but no graph database is introduced.
- No co-mention creates competition.
- No peer relationship is accepted without explicit evidence or a documented deterministic similarity policy.
- Evidence-backed competitors and algorithmically similar peers are separate concepts and must be labeled separately.
- Strict historical replay uses no-lookahead rules and complete lineage.
- Do not modify scoring, ranking, valuation, committee, Opportunity Timing, Market Validation, `EvidenceBackedProjectExecutor`, or canonical runtime behavior.

## Domain Model

### Core Concepts

- `PeerSet`: a versioned set of candidates comparable to a subject candidate under an explicit comparison scope.
- `PeerSetMember`: one member of a peer set with role, source, confidence, freshness, and lineage.
- `CompetitiveRelationship`: an evidence-backed relationship between two candidates or entities.
- `AlgorithmicPeerRelationship`: a deterministic similarity-derived relationship, not a competition claim.
- `ComparisonDimension`: a normalized dimension used to compare candidates.
- `CompetitiveAssessment`: a reportable current or point-in-time view of competitive context.

### Relationship Types

Evidence-backed relationship types:

- `direct_competitor`
- `indirect_competitor`
- `substitute`
- `centralized_incumbent`
- `open_source_alternative`
- `ecosystem_peer`
- `technology_peer`
- `category_peer`
- `use_case_peer`

Algorithmic relationship types:

- `same_category_similarity`
- `same_chain_similarity`
- `same_use_case_similarity`
- `same_protocol_type_similarity`
- `same_market_segment_similarity`

Algorithmic relationships must never be reported as evidence-backed competition.

### Comparison Dimensions

Supported dimensions:

- market category;
- protocol category;
- ecosystem;
- chain;
- technology stack;
- use case;
- user segment;
- asset/native-token distinction;
- protocol type;
- revenue model where evidence exists;
- liquidity venue coverage where evidence exists;
- developer ecosystem where evidence exists;
- deployment surface where evidence exists.

Unavailable dimensions remain unavailable.

## Evidence Semantics

### Accepted Evidence Sources

Competitive intelligence may use only:

- validated `KnowledgeClaim` records;
- eligible `KnowledgeRelationship` projections linked to claims;
- Candidate Registry identity records and source links;
- Identity Resolution outcomes;
- persisted Hunter evidence with point-in-time availability;
- official documentation, governance documentation, verified repositories, and existing provider evidence after v3.0.0 intake and validation.

### Predicate Use

The implementation must extend or use the Predicate Registry with versioned predicates such as:

- `competes_with`
- `substitutes_for`
- `same_category_as`
- `same_use_case_as`
- `targets_market_segment`
- `uses_technology`
- `runs_on`
- `integrates_with`
- `centralized_incumbent_of`

Predicate-specific conflict and compatibility rules must prevent false conflicts across different scopes, products, chains, time periods, and modalities.

### Competition vs Similarity

Evidence-backed competition requires an accepted claim or relationship with explicit support.

Algorithmic similarity may use deterministic policy over registry fields and validated claims, but it must:

- be labeled `algorithmic_similarity`;
- include policy id and version;
- include compared dimensions;
- preserve missing dimensions;
- not imply market competition;
- not feed investment scoring in this sprint.

Co-mention, ticker similarity, popularity, provider rank, and market-cap proximity are insufficient.

## Repository and Storage

Create an additive SQL-backed `hunter.competitive` package or similarly scoped module consistent with existing package conventions.

Required authoritative tables:

- `competitive_peer_sets`
- `competitive_peer_set_members`
- `competitive_relationships`
- `algorithmic_peer_relationships`
- `competitive_comparison_dimensions`
- `competitive_assessments`
- `competitive_relationship_evidence_links`
- `competitive_relationship_span_links`
- `peer_set_evidence_links`
- `peer_set_span_links`
- `competitive_conflict_links`
- `competitive_processing_runs`
- `competitive_checkpoints`

Required indexes:

- subject candidate plus status;
- peer candidate plus relationship type;
- peer set scope plus version;
- relationship type plus effective/recorded time;
- confidence, freshness, and coverage reporting;
- evidence id and span id lineage;
- processing run id;
- deterministic idempotency keys.

No multi-valued SQL-authoritative lineage fields may be stored as JSON/list blobs. JSON is permitted only for non-authoritative small metadata after indexed columns exist.

## Data Flow

```text
Candidate Registry
  + Identity/Trust outcomes
  + Evidence Intelligence claims and relationship projections
  -> Competitive input selection
  -> Evidence-backed relationship extraction
  -> Deterministic similarity policy evaluation
  -> Peer-set construction
  -> Confidence, coverage, freshness, and conflict projection
  -> SQL persistence with lineage
  -> Reports, CLI, automation, and future graph-ready consumers
```

## Class Boundaries

Expected production components:

- `CompetitiveRepository`: SQL-authoritative persistence and point-in-time queries.
- `CompetitiveInputSelector`: selects trusted candidates and evidence-backed claims without full registry scans.
- `CompetitiveRelationshipBuilder`: converts validated claims/projections into evidence-backed relationships.
- `AlgorithmicPeerPolicy`: deterministic, versioned similarity rules.
- `PeerSetBuilder`: creates scoped peer sets from evidence-backed and algorithmic relationships.
- `CompetitiveConfidenceProjector`: computes confidence from source authority, claim confidence, identity confidence, freshness, coverage, and conflicts.
- `CompetitiveConflictDetector`: detects contradictory competitive relationships using predicate-aware rules.
- `CompetitiveReporter`: reports coverage, peer sets, conflicts, lineage, missing evidence, and replay mode.
- `CompetitiveAutomationManager`: installs operational jobs into the existing Scheduler format.

No component may call scoring, Opportunity Timing, committee, Market Validation, or valuation paths.

## Confidence, Coverage, Freshness, and Conflicts

Every peer set and relationship must expose:

- confidence;
- confidence components;
- coverage by dimension;
- freshness;
- missing evidence;
- source authority status;
- identity status;
- supporting evidence ids;
- supporting span ids;
- conflict ids;
- policy id where algorithmic;
- strict replay mode.

Conflict detection must distinguish:

- conflicting competitor claims;
- obsolete/superseded claims;
- scope differences;
- chain or product differences;
- direct competition versus broad category similarity;
- active dispute versus resolved historical conflict.

## Historical Replay

All point-in-time queries must support:

- current mode;
- historical strict known-by-Hunter mode;
- reconstructed-after-cutoff mode.

Strict mode may use only:

- Candidate Registry state known at cutoff;
- Identity/Trust state known at cutoff;
- `KnowledgeClaim` lifecycle events with `effective_at <= cutoff` and `recorded_at <= cutoff`;
- document and authority states known at cutoff;
- relationship/conflict events known at cutoff;
- competitive records whose own effective and recorded times satisfy cutoff constraints.

Reconstruction mode must be explicitly labeled and never reported as knowledge known at cutoff.

## Automation

Install idempotent jobs using the existing Scheduler configuration model:

- competitive input refresh;
- evidence-backed relationship build;
- algorithmic peer-set refresh;
- conflict detection;
- competitive report generation.

Scheduler remains operational-only. Analytical dependency order belongs to the Competitive Intelligence pipeline components, not the Scheduler.

Jobs must be idempotent, checkpointed, non-overlapping, retry-safe, and safe after restart.

## Migration and Backward Compatibility

- Additive SQL schema only.
- No destructive migrations.
- No changes to Candidate Registry identity semantics.
- No changes to Evidence Intelligence truth semantics.
- No changes to scoring, ranking, valuation, committee, Opportunity Timing, Market Validation, or canonical runtime behavior.
- Existing commands and tests must remain backward compatible.
- Runtime data and generated reports must not be committed.

## Implementation Phases

### Phase 1: Models, Policies, and Predicate Extensions

Deliverables:

- Competitive domain models.
- Relationship and peer-set statuses.
- Deterministic id generation.
- Versioned `AlgorithmicPeerPolicy`.
- Predicate Registry extensions for competition and similarity.

Acceptance criteria:

- Evidence-backed and algorithmic relationships are separate types.
- Competition statuses and peer-set statuses are defined.
- No model contains SQL-authoritative list/blob lineage.
- Predicate extensions are versioned and graph-ready where eligible.

### Phase 2: SQL Repository and Migrations

Deliverables:

- SQL tables and normalized association tables.
- Indexed point-in-time and lineage queries.
- Idempotent write paths.

Acceptance criteria:

- Complete relationship and peer-set lineage reconstructs without JSON/list scans.
- Repeated writes are idempotent.
- Existing repositories are unchanged except for additive integration points.

### Phase 3: Input Selection from Registry, Trust, and Evidence Intelligence

Deliverables:

- Trusted candidate selector.
- Evidence claim selector.
- Relationship projection selector.
- Missing/unavailable input states.

Acceptance criteria:

- Unresolved or conflicted identity is excluded or explicitly marked unavailable.
- Current authority does not leak into historical replay.
- Input selection is incremental and indexed.

### Phase 4: Evidence-Backed Relationship Builder

Deliverables:

- Claim-to-competitive-relationship builder.
- Scope, qualifier, modality, and lifecycle handling.
- Evidence/span lineage persistence.

Acceptance criteria:

- Co-mentions never create competition.
- Only accepted claims/projections create evidence-backed relationships.
- Superseded, retracted, source-removed, disputed, unavailable, and rejected states are respected.

### Phase 5: Algorithmic Peer Policy

Deliverables:

- Deterministic similarity policy.
- Dimension comparison engine.
- Policy versioning and explainability.

Acceptance criteria:

- Algorithmic peers are never labeled competitors.
- Missing dimensions remain missing.
- Similarity is deterministic from the same inputs.

### Phase 6: Peer-Set Construction and Confidence

Deliverables:

- Peer-set builder.
- Confidence, coverage, freshness, and completeness projection.
- Conflict-aware peer-set status.

Acceptance criteria:

- Peer sets separate evidence-backed competitors from algorithmic peers.
- Confidence components are explainable.
- Coverage and freshness are computed from persisted evidence only.

### Phase 7: Conflict Detection and Historical Replay

Deliverables:

- Competitive conflict detection.
- Point-in-time competitive queries.
- Strict and reconstructed report modes.

Acceptance criteria:

- Different chains, scopes, products, or time periods are not false conflicts.
- Historical strict mode uses both effective and recorded time.
- Resolved or superseded conflicts preserve prior historical state.

### Phase 8: CLI, Reporting, Automation, and Documentation

Deliverables:

- Competitive CLI namespace.
- Coverage, peer, competitor, explain, conflict, and report outputs.
- Idempotent automation install/status.
- Documentation update for semantics and limitations.

Acceptance criteria:

- Reports expose lineage, missing evidence, conflicts, confidence, freshness, and replay mode.
- Automation jobs are valid existing Scheduler job definitions.
- No analytical logic is placed in Scheduler.

## Risks

- False competition from weak similarity: mitigated by strict labeling and evidence requirements.
- Peer-set explosion at market scale: mitigated by indexed candidate selection, scoped policies, batching, and incremental refresh.
- Lookahead leakage: mitigated by effective and recorded time constraints across all inputs.
- Boundary drift into scoring: mitigated by read-only reporting and no scoring integration in this sprint.
- Over-abstracted graph design: mitigated by SQL authority and graph-ready projections only.

## Completion Gate

Sprint v3.1.0 is complete only when:

- evidence-backed competitors are persisted separately from algorithmic peers;
- every competitive relationship has lineage or policy provenance;
- peer sets expose confidence, coverage, freshness, conflicts, and missing evidence;
- strict no-lookahead replay works end to end;
- reports explain whether a relationship is evidence-backed or algorithmic;
- automation installs valid Scheduler jobs idempotently;
- no scoring, ranking, valuation, committee, Opportunity Timing, Market Validation, or canonical runtime behavior changes;
- all required tests and quality gates pass.
