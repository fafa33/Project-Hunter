# Sprint v2.9.0: Identity Resolution Foundation

## Status

- Release: `v2.9.0`
- Status: approved
- Release tag: `v2.9.0`

## Mission

Implement the first production Identity Resolution layer for the Candidate Registry.

Hunter now discovers hundreds of candidates from market-cap, protocol, and DEX-market sources. The next highest-value architectural step is to resolve which source records represent the same economic entity, which records are ambiguous, and which records must remain separate.

The sprint must make Hunter safer and more useful for market-wide discovery without implementing intrinsic value, competition intelligence, network effects, or portfolio logic.

## Primary Goal

Convert discovered records into deterministic, evidence-backed identity outcomes while preserving unresolved ambiguity.

Hunter must distinguish:

- exact identity matches;
- probable matches;
- ambiguous matches;
- conflicts;
- rejected identities;
- unresolved records.

## In Scope

- Identity Resolution service for Candidate Registry records.
- Deterministic resolution rules based on approved evidence.
- Resolution outcome persistence.
- Conflict persistence and reporting.
- Merge recommendations where evidence is sufficient.
- Safe canonical candidate preservation.
- Contract-based matching across providers.
- Trusted provider-id matching where configured evidence supports it.
- Official domain matching when verified.
- Official repository matching when verified.
- Native asset versus token distinction fields required for future phases.
- Wrapped and bridged representation handling structures.
- Candidate lifecycle integration from `discovered` or `screenable` into `identified` where evidence supports it.
- CLI/reporting for identity coverage, ambiguity, conflicts, rejected records, and unresolved records.
- Deterministic tests for collision, ambiguity, conflict, and exact-match behavior.

## Out of Scope

- Intrinsic Value.
- Competition Intelligence.
- Network Effects.
- Machine Learning.
- Portfolio Engine.
- REST API.
- Dashboard redesign.
- Investment scoring changes.
- Opportunity Timing algorithm changes.
- Any change to `EvidenceBackedProjectExecutor`.

## Architecture Constraints

- Do not redesign the Discovery Layer.
- Do not replace the Candidate Registry.
- Do not merge by ticker equality.
- Do not infer identity from popularity, market cap, or wallet behavior.
- Do not fabricate official domains, repositories, contracts, or aliases.
- Preserve point-in-time truth.
- Preserve raw source records and source references.
- Preserve unresolved ambiguity instead of forcing a merge.

## Acceptable Identity Evidence

High-confidence evidence:

- exact verified contract plus chain;
- official domain;
- official repository;
- trusted configured provider id;
- verified migration record;
- protocol-owned documentation;
- existing persisted Hunter evidence.

Weak evidence that must not independently merge candidates:

- ticker equality;
- similar names;
- similar logos;
- similar categories;
- market-cap rank proximity;
- social-media claims without official verification;
- exchange listings without canonical identity evidence.

## Required Outcomes

Every identity evaluation must produce one of:

- `exact`
- `probable`
- `ambiguous`
- `conflict`
- `rejected`
- `unresolved`

Every outcome must persist:

- candidate id;
- source candidate ids;
- evidence ids or source ids;
- confidence;
- reason;
- detected conflicts;
- missing evidence;
- evaluated timestamp.

## Performance Requirements

The resolver must be designed for at least 100,000 candidates.

Requirements:

- use indexed identifier, contract, domain, repository, and source lookups;
- avoid full registry scans in normal incremental operation;
- support batch resolution;
- support incremental resolution after new provider sync;
- keep operations idempotent;
- avoid O(n²) pairwise candidate comparison.

## Reporting Requirements

Add or extend reports for:

- identity coverage;
- exact matches;
- probable matches;
- ambiguous records;
- conflicts;
- rejected records;
- unresolved records;
- ticker collisions;
- contract collisions;
- provider disagreements;
- candidates blocked from screening or deep analysis by identity ambiguity.

## Acceptance Criteria

- Identity Resolution service implemented without architecture redesign.
- Deterministic exact/probable/ambiguous/conflict/rejected/unresolved outcomes.
- Ticker collisions remain unresolved unless stronger evidence exists.
- Contract matches are chain-aware.
- Provider disagreements are persisted and reported.
- Existing v2.8.0 discovery behavior remains backward compatible.
- Candidate Registry remains idempotent.
- All quality gates pass.
- Live discovery validation still succeeds for configured public providers where the environment permits access.
