# Project Hunter Constitution

## Purpose

This Constitution is the highest governing document of Project Hunter.

It defines the permanent constitutional rules that every architectural decision, implementation, governance process, and future evolution of the project must respect.

Every lower-level document derives its authority from this Constitution and must remain consistent with it.

---

# Constitutional Rule 1 — Purpose

Project Hunter exists to produce trustworthy, evidence-driven investment decision support through continuous market discovery, analytical reasoning, and explainable intelligence.

Its purpose is to improve long-term investment decisions rather than maximize software complexity, feature count, or analytical volume.

---

# Constitutional Rule 2 — Evidence Authority

Evidence is the foundation of every authoritative conclusion.

No analytical output may become authoritative unless it is supported by traceable, reproducible, and verifiable evidence.

Unknown information remains unknown.

Missing information remains missing.

Conflicting information remains conflicting until resolved.

---

# Constitutional Rule 3 — Deterministic Intelligence

Authoritative intelligence must be reproducible.

The same evidence, methodology, and configuration must always produce the same analytical result.

Historical decisions must remain reproducible from information available at the historical point in time.

---

# Constitutional Rule 4 — Architectural Integrity

Project Hunter evolves through disciplined architecture.

Architectural boundaries, responsibilities, and ownership must remain explicit.

Architectural convenience is never sufficient justification for violating established boundaries.

---

# Constitutional Rule 5 — Single Source of Truth

Every architectural concept, analytical authority, and governance rule must have one canonical owner.

Competing authorities, duplicated ownership, and conflicting definitions are prohibited.

Supporting documents may reference canonical concepts but must not redefine them.

---

# Constitutional Rule 6 — Explainability

Every authoritative analytical conclusion must be explainable.

Hunter must preserve the reasoning, evidence, methodology, uncertainty, and provenance supporting every meaningful analytical output.

Opaque authority is prohibited.

---

# Constitutional Rule 7 — Long-Term Evolution

Project Hunter is designed as a long-lived analytical platform.

Every significant decision should improve:

- maintainability;
- extensibility;
- reliability;
- auditability;
- historical correctness;
- future adaptability.

Short-term convenience must never compromise long-term integrity.

---

# Constitutional Rule 8 — Governance

Architectural, implementation, and operational decisions must follow the established governance hierarchy.

No lower-level document may contradict this Constitution.

Conflicts between documents are resolved by the canonical authority hierarchy.

---

# Constitutional Rule 9 — Constitutional Change

This Constitution may evolve only through explicit constitutional revision.

Architectural convenience, implementation difficulty, release pressure, or local optimization are not sufficient justification for violating constitutional requirements.

Constitutional changes should occur only when they demonstrably improve the long-term correctness, integrity, or sustainability of Project Hunter.

---
