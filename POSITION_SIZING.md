# POSITION SIZING

## Purpose

The Position Sizing Engine determines how much capital should be allocated to each investment.

Finding an exceptional opportunity is not enough.

Correct position sizing is equally important.

The objective is maximizing long-term Expected Asymmetric Return while minimizing permanent capital loss.

---

# First Principle

Conviction alone is never enough.

Position size must be determined by evidence.

Never allocate capital based on emotion.

---

# Position Size Inputs

Every allocation must consider:

Hunter Score

Confidence Score

Probability Model

Risk Score

Liquidity

Market Cycle

Macro Environment

Tokenomics

Smart Money Score

Narrative Score

---

# Position Classes

Every investment belongs to one class.

Class S

Exceptional Opportunity

Highest Conviction

Maximum allocation allowed.

---

Class A

High Conviction

Strong evidence

Large allocation.

---

Class B

Good Opportunity

Medium allocation.

---

Class C

Speculative Opportunity

Small allocation.

---

Class D

Experimental Position

Very small allocation.

---

Reject

No allocation.

---

# Allocation Rules

Higher conviction does not automatically mean larger allocation.

Risk must always be considered.

Allocation increases only when:

Evidence improves.

Risk declines.

Confidence increases.

Liquidity is sufficient.

---

# Scaling In

Preferred strategy:

Enter gradually.

Never deploy full capital immediately.

Increase exposure only when evidence improves.

Average up is acceptable if supported by stronger fundamentals.

Average down requires exceptional justification.

---

# Scaling Out

Reduce exposure when:

Investment thesis weakens.

Market reprices significantly.

Risk increases.

Better opportunities appear.

Portfolio concentration becomes excessive.

---

# Concentration Risk

No single investment should dominate the portfolio without extraordinary evidence.

Concentration requires:

Highest Hunter Score

Highest Confidence

Strongest probability profile

Excellent liquidity

Exceptional asymmetric return

---

# Liquidity Adjustment

Illiquid assets require smaller positions.

Position size should decrease when:

Trading volume is low.

Order books are thin.

Exit risk increases.

---

# Volatility Adjustment

Higher volatility requires smaller allocation.

Volatility alone is not risk.

Permanent capital loss is risk.

---

# Position Review

Every position must be reviewed regularly.

Questions:

Does this position still deserve its current size?

Would I buy this amount today?

Has evidence improved?

Has evidence weakened?

---

# Maximum Loss

Before opening a position determine:

Maximum acceptable loss.

Conditions for reducing exposure.

Conditions for exiting completely.

---

# Opportunity Cost

Every position competes with every other opportunity.

Capital should migrate toward stronger evidence.

---

# Final Principle

Position size is a function of evidence, probability and risk.

Never confuse conviction with certainty.

Capital allocation is one of the most important investment decisions.
