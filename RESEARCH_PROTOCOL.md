# RESEARCH PROTOCOL

## Purpose

This document defines the mandatory research process of Project Hunter.

Every project must pass through exactly the same research process.

No shortcuts are allowed.

No conclusions are allowed before completing every stage.

Research must always be systematic.

---

# First Principle

Research starts with questions.

Not with answers.

The objective is not proving a thesis.

The objective is discovering the truth.

---

# Phase 1 — Discovery

Collect basic information.

Project Name

Ticker

Sector

Sub-sector

Launch Date

Current Market Cap

FDV

Current Price

ATH

ATL

Official Website

Official Documentation

GitHub

Social Channels

Whitepaper

---

# Phase 2 — Product

Understand the product.

What problem does it solve?

Who needs it?

Who pays for it?

Does it have real customers?

Does it generate revenue?

Is the product working today?

Is it technically superior?

Can competitors easily replace it?

---

# Phase 3 — Market

Determine the addressable market.

How large is the opportunity?

Who are the competitors?

Is the market growing?

Is the market saturated?

Is the project a market leader?

Can the market support a 10x increase in valuation?

---

# Phase 4 — Token

Analyze the token.

Why does the token exist?

Is the token necessary?

Does demand increase with adoption?

Does revenue flow back to the token?

Who captures the value?

Would the business still work without the token?

---

# Phase 5 — Team

Research the founders.

Background

Previous companies

Previous exits

Reputation

Execution history

Transparency

Developer quality

Community trust

Users

Revenue

# Phase 8 — Smart Money

Investigate:

VC ownership

Developer wallets

Exchange flows

Long-term holders

---

# Phase 9 — Historical Comparison

Compare the project with previous winners.

Do NOT compare with today's version.

Compare with their early stage.

Examples:

Bitcoin

Ethereum

BNB

Chainlink

Solana

Render

Maker

Aave

Injective

Kaspa

Hyperliquid

API3

DigiByte

---

# Phase 10 — Bear Case

Before writing a bullish thesis,

build the strongest bearish thesis possible.

Answer:

Why will this project fail?

Why is the market correct?

Why are professional investors ignoring it?

What assumptions are probably wrong?

What would destroy the investment thesis?

---

# Phase 11 — Bull Case

Only after surviving the Bear Case.

Explain:

Why is the market wrong?

Why could the project succeed?

What evidence supports this?

Which catalyst could change market perception?

---

# Phase 12 — Probability

Estimate probabilities.

2x

5x

10x

20x

50x

100x

Permanent failure

Every probability must include an explanation.

Never provide numbers without reasoning.

---

# Phase 13 — Final Decision

Possible outcomes:

Reject

Watchlist

Monitor

Research Again

Candidate

High Conviction

Exceptional Asymmetry

Every decision must explain:

Why.

Based on what evidence.

What information is still missing.

---

# Research Quality Checklist

Before publishing a report verify:

All major data collected.

Bear thesis completed.

Bull thesis completed.

Tokenomics analyzed.

Smart money analyzed.

Historical comparison completed.

Risk analysis completed.

Confidence score assigned.

Unknown information identified.

No unsupported assumptions remain.

---

# Final Principle

The quality of Hunter is determined by the quality of its research.

Research is never finished.

Research only becomes more accurate as new evidence appears.
