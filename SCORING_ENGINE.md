# SCORING ENGINE

## Purpose

The Scoring Engine transforms raw evidence into a quantitative investment score.

The objective is NOT to identify good projects.

The objective is to identify projects that are most likely being materially underpriced by the market.

Every score must be explainable.

Every score must be reproducible.

Every score must be evidence-based.

---

# First Principle

Scores never represent certainty.

Scores represent probabilities.

A higher score means:

Higher probability that the market is significantly underpricing the project.

---

# Evidence Categories

The scoring engine evaluates evidence from independent categories.

Examples include:

Market Mispricing

Historical Similarity

Tokenomics

Developer Strength

Revenue

Adoption

Smart Money

On-chain Activity

Narrative Timing

Catalysts

Macro Alignment

Risk

Each category is evaluated independently.

---

# Evidence Quality

Every observation receives a confidence level.

High Confidence

Medium Confidence

Low Confidence

Unknown

Low confidence evidence contributes less.

Unknown evidence contributes nothing.

---

# Positive Signals

Examples include:

Growing revenue

Increasing users

Developer growth

Whale accumulation

Improving tokenomics

Enterprise adoption

Strong network effects

Supply reduction

Growing ecosystem

Historical similarity to previous winners

---

# Negative Signals

Examples include:

Unlimited inflation

Heavy unlock schedules

Weak token utility

Centralization

Declining developers

Declining users

Revenue deterioration

Broken governance

Poor security

Narrative exhaustion

---

# Unknown Signals

Unknown data must never be estimated.

Unknown data reduces confidence.

Unknown data is never treated as positive.

---

# Contradictions

Contradictory evidence must remain visible.

Example:

Excellent technology

+

Poor tokenomics

The engine must preserve both observations.

Never average contradictions away.

---

# Confidence Score

Final confidence depends on:

Data quality

Data freshness

Independent confirmation

Historical consistency

Amount of missing information

Confidence must always be reported separately from score.

---

# Final Hunter Score

The final score combines:

Evidence Quality

Evidence Quantity

Historical Validation

Market Mispricing

Risk

Confidence

No single factor may dominate permanently.

The engine must continuously rebalance itself through historical validation.

---

# Decision Levels

Hunter Score does NOT automatically mean BUY.

Instead classify:

Reject

Watchlist

Monitor

Research Required

Candidate

High Conviction Candidate

Exceptional Asymmetry Candidate

Every classification must include a written explanation.

---

# Explainability

Every score must answer:

Why did this project receive this score?

Which evidence increased the score?

Which evidence reduced the score?

What information is still missing?

What could invalidate the thesis?

No black-box decisions are allowed.

---

# Continuous Learning

After every completed market cycle:

Compare prediction versus reality.

Measure errors.

Adjust feature importance.

Improve future scoring.

The scoring engine must become more accurate after every cycle.

---

# Final Principle

A score without explanation has no value.

Evidence always overrides assumptions.

Historical validation always overrides confidence.
